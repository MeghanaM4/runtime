# Submission criteria

Make a [GitHub repo](https://github.com) for your runtime project, and commit progress often! You also need to track the time you've spent on your Runtime board. Here are your two options:

### Option A: Journaling

Keep a running log of what you worked on each session, titled by the date and how long you worked for. Each entry should have pictures and/or videos of what you accomplished. A markdown file (<span class="inline-code">.md</span>) in your repo works best for this. You should be journaling every 4-6 hours.

### Option B: Lookout

[Lookout](https://lookout.hackclub.com) is Hack Club's timelapse-based time tracker. Download the desktop app, and start a session every time you open KiCAD.

*P.S. You can also use both methods if you want! It'll make you get your grant faster and will reduce the chance of your project being rejected.*

# How to journal
And when you sit down to journal, write it like you're explaining the session to someone else, not marking a timestamp. <span class="inline-code">Worked on schematic, 1h</span> tells nobody anything. What did you actually figure out? What are you stuck on? Every entry should have 2-4 screenshots of what you worked on, too (this is mega helpful when you're trying to look back on what your thought process was if you encounter any mistakes).
