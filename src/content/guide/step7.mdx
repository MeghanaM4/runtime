First, get your manufacturing files. Open your PCB Editor, go to **File → Fabrication Outputs → Gerbers (.gbr)**, and hit Plot. Then go to **File → Export → Fabrication Outputs → Drill Files (.dril)** and generate those into the same folder. Zip the whole folder up.

<img src="https://pathfinder.hackclub.com/public/stepimgs/step6.1.gif"></img>

Go to [JLCPCB.com](https://jlcpcb.com/), make an account, hit "Order now," and upload your zip using "Add gerber file." If a preview of your board doesn't show up, you probably didn't generate the right files.

Change your PCB thickness to **0.8mm**, it makes the board way cheaper. Changing the color of the board is cool, but the production time will take longer (like 2 more days).

Get the minimum number of boards (5), and if you're getting PCBA, get the minimum of assembled boards (2).

## PCB Assembly

If you have a ton of SMD parts (or parts that are super small and you don't have a hotplate), you can get JLCPCB to assemble the board for you.

- When setting up your order, opt into **PCB Assembly**.
- You'll upload a **BOM** (bill of materials) and a **placement file** , a spreadsheet of each part's designator, X/Y position, rotation, and which side of the board it's on. KiCAD exports this directly: **File → Fabrication Outputs → Component Placement Files**.
- Check the **rotation** on every single part in JLCPCB's placement preview before confirming.
- Only parts sourced from JLCPCB's own catalog with Economic or Standard PCBA status can get assembled this way. Anything you're hand-soldering yourself (headers, switches, breakout boards) should be marked **DNP** (do not place) or left off the placement file entirely.

<div class="callout stop">
<span class="label">Stay right side up</span>
<p>A part placed backward (especially your microcontroller or anything polarized) usually means a dead board when it shows up. Zoom into JLCPCB's placement preview and check every rotation before you confirm the order.</p>
</div>

## Check out

JLCPCB will make you order 3 non-assembled boards with 2 assembled boards, this is the minimum. When you're ordering, see if you have any coupons and click the cheapest shipping option.
